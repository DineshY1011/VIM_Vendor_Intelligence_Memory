[00:00 — 00:04] — Introductions and agenda

Jordan Mills: Good morning everyone, thanks for making the time.
Today we want to walk through your account health, discuss the
renewal coming up in June, and answer any questions you have on
the new pricing in the renewal doc we sent in February.

Priya Ramesh: Thanks Jordan. We have a few items to cover.
Ravi and Arjun have both flagged concerns I want to raise directly.

-------------------------------------------------------------------
[00:04 — 00:18] — Pricing discussion

Ravi Shankar: Jordan, the renewal doc shows $0.0081 per SMS.
Our original contract in 2022 was $0.0075. That is an 8% increase.
We were never formally notified about that change. When did that
happen?

Jordan Mills: That rate adjustment happened in late 2022 as part
of a broader India carrier cost revision. I understand the
communication could have been clearer. I apologise for that.

Ravi Shankar: Clearer? We received no notice at all. Our contract
says 60 days written notice is required before any rate change.
That did not happen. We have been paying the higher rate for
over a year without agreeing to it.

Jordan Mills: I hear you, Ravi. I want to make this right.
I cannot retroactively reverse 16 months of invoicing, but I can
commit to holding the current rate of $0.0081 through the end of
Q2 2024 — that is, through June 30 — while you evaluate the
renewal terms.

Priya Ramesh: I need that in writing, Jordan.

Jordan Mills: Absolutely, I will follow up with a written
confirmation by end of this week.

-------------------------------------------------------------------
[00:18 — 00:31] — SLA and outage discussion

Arjun Menon: I want to raise the outage from June 2023.
We were down for over four hours. We filed a formal credit claim
on June 16, 2023. We never received the credit. Ravi followed up
with you directly. No response after your initial acknowledgement.

Jordan Mills: I am really sorry about that. Let me look into the
credit status after this call. That should not have fallen through.

Sarah Okafor: I will pick that up as an action item, Arjun.
I will confirm the credit status and get back to you by end of
next week.

Arjun Menon: Also, the renewal document removes the 99.95% uptime
guarantee entirely. Section 3.1 in the old contract had a specific
uptime SLA with a credit schedule. The new document just says
"commercially reasonable efforts". That is not acceptable to us.

Jordan Mills: That is a standard clause update we have rolled out
across our enterprise contracts. The intent is the same, but I
understand the concern. I can flag internally whether we can
reinstate specific uptime language for your account.

Priya Ramesh: Please do. We cannot sign a renewal without a
defined uptime commitment and a credit mechanism. That is a
non-starter for us.

-------------------------------------------------------------------
[00:31 — 00:44] — Volume and growth discussion

Jordan Mills: I also wanted to discuss your volume trajectory.
You are running about 1.4 to 1.5 million SMS per month. We expect

Arjun Menon: We expect to hit that threshold by Q3 2024. Is that

Jordan Mills: It is not explicitly in the current draft, but I can

SMS in a calendar month, the rate automatically drops to $0.0077

Priya Ramesh: That needs to be in writing in the agreement, not a
verbal commitment.
